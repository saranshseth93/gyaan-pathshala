# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Indian parents (primarily mothers) in Ahmedabad, Gujarat, searching for tuition classes for children aged 3–12 (Nursery to Class 7). They browse on budget Android phones over mobile data. Many are bilingual (Hindi/Gujarati + English). They compare 3–5 local tuition options before deciding, often via WhatsApp forwards from other parents.

Their job: find a tuition that feels safe, personal, and proven — not a factory. They want to see the teacher's face, understand the approach, and contact instantly via WhatsApp or phone.

## Product Purpose

Single-page marketing site for Gyaan Pathshala, a small-batch tuition center in Paldi, Ahmedabad run by Bina Tejura (12+ years experience). The site exists to convert browsing parents into WhatsApp inquiries or phone calls. Success = parent picks up the phone or sends a WhatsApp message within 60 seconds of landing.

Email and physical address are not primary contact channels. WhatsApp and phone are the only CTAs that matter.

## Brand Personality

**Nurturing, credible, spirited.**

- Nurturing: the warmth of a teacher who knows your child's name
- Credible: 12 years of results, not marketing fluff
- Spirited: learning is joyful here, not a grind

Voice is warm and direct. Speaks like a trusted neighbor who happens to be an excellent teacher, not like a corporate institution. Occasional Hindi/Gujarati touches feel natural, not forced.

## Anti-references

- Byju's, Vedantu, WhiteHat Jr, and similar ed-tech platforms: too corporate, too tech-forward, too slick. Gyaan Pathshala is a neighborhood tuition, not a startup.
- Generic coaching center sites with stock photos, clip art, and cramped layouts.
- Template sites that could belong to any business in any city.
- Cartoon-heavy "kids" aesthetics with rainbow colors and Comic Sans energy.
- Dark/moody tech aesthetics. This is about children and sunlight.

The site should feel like it could only belong to one specific tuition in Paldi, Ahmedabad. Not interchangeable.

## Design Principles

1. **Neighborhood, not corporation.** Every element should feel personal and local. A parent should sense a real person behind this, not a marketing team.
2. **Trust through specificity.** Specific numbers, real photos, named teacher. Vague claims erode trust; concrete details build it.
3. **Phone-first, always.** Every design decision optimized for a 6-inch Android screen on slow data. Desktop is a bonus, not the target.
4. **Joy without noise.** Playful and warm, but never cluttered or overwhelming. White space is kindness on a small screen.
5. **One tap to talk.** WhatsApp and phone CTAs visible at all times. Never more than one thumb-reach away.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Optimize for low-bandwidth: compress images aggressively, minimize JS, lazy-load below fold
- Touch targets minimum 44x44px for mobile
- Support reduced motion preferences
- Text must be readable at default Android font sizes without zooming
- Bilingual consideration: UI in English but brand touches in Hindi/Gujarati should render correctly (Devanagari and Gujarati script support)
- High contrast for outdoor/bright sunlight viewing on phone screens
